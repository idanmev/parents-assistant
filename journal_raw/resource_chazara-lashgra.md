# Coaching Resource: Return to Routine (חזרה לשגרה)

**Author:** Natascha Sigolovich  
**For:** Idan (returning from time away) + Maya's re-stabilization  
**Title:** Summary and Important Notes — For Idan's Stable Return and Better Regulation for Maya

---

## 1. Don't Take the Resistance Personally

Maya is not rejecting you — she simply adapted to Sveta's rhythm, language, and style during the period you weren't home. Now that you're returning, her nervous system needs time to readjust.

**Important to remember:** Her reactions are not against you. They are part of the adaptation process.

When you stay calm, consistent, and unfazed — you teach her security.

---

## 2. Advance Preparation: Who Organizes Maya Morning/Evening

Before each day, tell Maya:
> "Today Dad/Mom will organize you. Each does things a bit differently, and you can explain to us what feels nice and what helps you. What you like and what you like less."

This gives her:
- Sense of control
- Opportunity to teach both parents what works for her
- Smoother transition between different styles

You can even teach her actual phrases:
- "Dad, I like it when..."
- "Mom, helps me when..."

This way you make her a partner — and resistance drops.

---

## 3. Parental Coordination — Your Conversation with Sveta Behind the Scenes

You and Sveta need to sit together, and she'll share 3-4 starting points that worked in the recent period:
- How Maya likes to get organized
- What calms her
- What triggers her
- What morning/evening order works for her

This way both of you give her the same "stable ground," even if each does it in their own style.

**Goal:** Fewer gaps — more cooperation.

---

## 4. Strengthening the Connection — Rebuilding "Dad's Figure" in Her Mind

For Maya to feel close and safe with you again, the connection needs to be built gradually. Sveta can tell her daily:
- "Dad misses you."
- "Dad loves to pamper you with ___."
- "Dad waited to do ___ together."

Your pain as a dad naturally rises. You, for your part, need to give her **short daily quality time**:
- 10–15 minutes walk
- Reading a book
- Talking about the day
- A small game
- A ride together with music

**Most important — every day, even if short. Later you can extend the time.**

---

## Summary

You are in a natural transition period. All you need to do:
- **Consistency**
- **Advance preparation**
- **Calm dialogue**
- **Short quality time**
- **Full cooperation between parents**
